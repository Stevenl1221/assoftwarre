Introduction

This privacy statement applies to the www.as-software.com website and its derivative websites, including, but not limited to www.ultrasoundreporting.com (collectively, Website(s)). It does not apply to any linked websites. To learn about the privacy policy of a linked website, please refer to the privacy statement for that particular website.

Effective Date

This Website privacy statement is effective as of January 1, 2011. We reserve the right to change this statement. If we change it, we will post the current privacy statement on this website. Any changes to this Website privacy statement will be effective as of the day posted.

Visiting Our Website

You can browse our Website without providing us with any personal information. You also may be able to set your browser to reject cookies. If you do that, our Website may be less functional for you.

There are times when you may choose to give us personal information about you. For example, you may request product information, for a representative to call you or you may request a demonstration of a new product. We need certain information from you so we can respond to your requests.

The Information We May Collect

We collect Personal Information that you voluntarily give us and Website Visitor data, on the Website(s).

Personal Information

We collect Personal Information that you give us, for example, to request a call from an AS Software representative, register for a training webinar, or complete a survey. This information commonly includes your name, the name of your organization, address, email address, telephone number, and, if applicable, your account number. Our website has security safeguards that are designed to protect the security of any personal information that you decide to give us via the Internet.

Website Visitor Data

Website Visitor Data is data about the way a website is navigated that is not associated with an individual's identity. We may collect this information to analyze how our Websites are used and for improvement. The kind of information we may collect includes: the pages visited on our website; IP addresses, the features used; how long a visitor stays; browser type, the domain name of the site from which visitors connect to our site and other navigational data. Most Visitor Data is collected using cookies.

Cookies are small pieces of information that a website sends to a computer's hard drive while the visitor is viewing a website. We may use both session Cookies (which expire once a visitor closes his/her web browser) and persistent Cookies (which stay on a visitor's computer until the visitor deletes them) to provide our Website visitors with a more personal and interactive experience. Persistent Cookies can be removed by following Internet browser help file directions. If a visitor chooses to disable Cookies, some areas of our website may not work properly.

How We May Use Information

We may use Personal Information collected on the Websites in several ways, for example, to respond to your request for a call, demonstration or referral, to tell you about products or services we offer, to authenticate clients, to third parties to provide you with access to the features and functions of the website. We use Website Visitor data to develop, manage, and improve our website. Sometimes we may add Website Visitor data to Personal Information from clients. We may use that information to: manage the Websites, help resolve problems, analyze use of the Websites, to third parties to provide you with access to the features and functions of the Websites, and tell you about products or services we offer.

From time to time, we may have subsidiaries, joint ventures, or other companies under common control with us (collectively, "Affiliates"). At present we do not share your Personal Information or Website Visitor data with our Affiliates, however if we do share some or all of Your Personal Information or Website data with any Affiliate, We will require that Affiliate to honor our Internet privacy statement. If AS Software or its assets are acquired by another company, that company will possess the Personal Information and Website Visitor data collected by us and it will assume the rights and obligations regarding Your Personal Information as described in our Internet privacy statement.

Regardless of any choices You make regarding Your Personal Information (as described below), We may disclose Your Personal Information if We believe in good faith that such disclosure is necessary to (a) comply with relevant laws or to respond to court orders; or (b) to protect and defend the rights or property of Us or third parties.
